About me


I am a third year PhD candidate in Mathematics working under the supervision of Christian Wuthrich at the University of Nottingham.

Previously, I did an MSc in Mathematics at the Autonomous University of Madrid. My thesis was titled Mazur's Control Theorem and was supervised by Daniel Macías Castillo. Before that, I did a double BSc in Mathematics and Physics at the Complutense University of Madrid.

My research interests are in Iwasawa theory. More specifically:
  • Relation between Kolyvagin systems and Selmer structures of core rank 0.
  • Kato's Euler system and applications to study the rank and Tate-Shafarevich groups of elliptic curves.


I can be contacted at alberto.angurelandres@nottingham.ac.uk.
